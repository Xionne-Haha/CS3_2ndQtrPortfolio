Adventure Games

Adventure games are a video game genre in which the player assumes the role of a protagonist in an interactive story, driven by exploration and/or puzzle-solving. The genre's focus on story allows it to draw heavily from other narrative-based media, such as literature and film, encompassing a wide variety of genres. Most adventure games (text and graphic) are designed for a single player, since the emphasis on story and character makes multiplayer design difficult.

Adventure games were initially developed in the 1970s and early 1980s as text-based interactive stories, using text parsers to translate the player's commands into actions. As personal computers became more powerful with better graphics, the graphic adventure-game format became popular, initially by augmenting player's text commands with graphics, but soon moving towards point-and-click interfaces. Further computer advances led to adventure games with more immersive graphics using real-time or pre-rendered three-dimensional scenes or full-motion video taken from the first- or third-person perspective.

Notable Examples

Red Dead Redemption 2 (2018)

Red Dead Redemption 2 is often considered as one of the greatest adventure games of its time. It's a 2018 action-adventure game (mostly adventure) developed and published by Rockstar Games. The game is set in an open world space with many quests to take on and the liberty that it grants players to do whatever they want. However, it includes quests that guide the players where to go and advance to the next milestone.

Myst (1993)

Myst is renowned for its atmospheric environments and challenging puzzles. The game invites players to explore mysterious islands, decipher clues, and piece together a story through exploration and observation. Its unique approach to gameplay and storytelling has made it a classic in the adventure genre.
